WHAT IS VPD & HOW TO READ THIS

VPD = how thirsty the air is. It rolls temperature and humidity into one number: how strongly the air pulls water vapour out of the leaf. That pull is what drives the plant to drink through its roots and "breathe" through its leaves.

🌬️ Think of a wet towel. In a warm, dry room it dries fast (high VPD). In a cool, steamy bathroom it stays damp (low VPD). Your plant's leaf is that towel.

Too low (blue): air too humid, barely transpires — sluggish growth, mould risk.
In band (green): healthy transpiration and nutrient uptake.
Too high (red): air too dry, the plant shuts its pores, growth stalls.

We use leaf temperature (air + offset), not air temp, because evaporation happens at the leaf surface — that's what pro controllers do. The heatmap shows every temp/humidity combo that lands you in the green for the selected stage.

PPFD vs DLI — PLAINLY EXPLAINED

PPFD is how bright the light is right now at the canopy — useful photons hitting a square metre each second. An instant reading.

DLI is the total light over a whole day — brightness × hours the light is on.

🌧️ Rain analogy: PPFD is how hard it's raining. DLI is how much ended up in the bucket by night. A drizzle all day or a short downpour can fill the same bucket.

Two rooms with the same PPFD can feed plants differently: 600 PPFD for 18 h ≫ 600 PPFD for 12 h. Plants care about the daily total, so that's the big gauge. The Light Converter below works any direction — fix what you want to solve for (DLI, PPFD or hours) and it computes the missing one live.

HOW THE TIMELINE IS BUILT

Day 1 is yours to define: seed popped (the taproot breaks the shell — true germination) or sprout up (the seedling breaks the surface, ~3 days later). Pick whichever you actually log.

Photoperiod plants flower when you cut light to 12 h ("the flip"): germination → seedling → veg (until your flip) → ~3-week stretch → flowering → flush → harvest, then a ~3-week cure.

Autoflowers ignore light hours and flower on their own clock — no flip — so the whole seed-to-harvest length comes from the strain.

The harvest window is the estimate ±5 days. The real call is trichomes: clear → cloudy → amber under a loupe. Everything saves in this browser.

WHAT THIS DOES & DOESN'T COUNT

Yield is estimated from your light wattage (× grams-per-watt) or from canopy area (× grams-per-m²) — or type a real number to override. The ±range reflects how much these rules of thumb swing in practice.

Cost per gram = total cycle cost ÷ yield. HOME counts electricity + nutrients only — the real out-of-pocket spend. COMMERCIAL adds rent, labour, and equipment spread over its useful life (upfront ÷ cycles).

Electricity assumes the light runs your set hours and other gear runs 24 h. It ignores seasonal HVAC swings, water, and your time unless you put a number on labour. Treat it as a solid planning estimate, not an accountant's ledger.

HOW BREAK-EVEN WORKS

You start in the red by your upfront spend. Each cycle adds your net profit, so the line climbs. Where it crosses zero is break-even — the setup has paid for itself.

Return / year is annual profit ÷ upfront cost — a quick yardstick vs other ways you could use the money. Pull your real profit/cycle from the ECON tab for an honest number.

It assumes steady output and price. One bad cycle pushes break-even further out — treat it as a plan, not a promise.
